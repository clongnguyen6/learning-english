# Present Perfect

Present perfect connects a past action to a present result or experience.

> Use it when the exact past time is not the focus.

## Structure

- Subject + have/has + past participle
- Negative: Subject + have/has not + past participle
- Question: Have/Has + subject + past participle?

## When To Use It

1. Experience without a specific time
2. A result that matters now
3. Recent news with `just`, `already`, or `yet`

## Examples

- I have visited Da Nang twice.
- She has already finished the grammar lesson.
- Have they submitted the reading assignment yet?

## Common Mistakes

- Do not use a finished-time marker such as `yesterday` with present perfect.
- Do not highlight inside `code spans` when keyword matching runs later.

## Quick Note

Present perfect often appears next to adverbs such as `ever`, `never`, `already`, and `yet`.
